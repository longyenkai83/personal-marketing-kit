# Style Guide — Marie Forleo Listicle Voice

Extracted from "12 Entrepreneurial Mistakes You Can't Afford to Make in 2026" (9-page lead magnet, Apr 2026). Use as the default voice when user hasn't supplied brand voice samples.

## Voice archetype
**The Mentor-Sister.** Warm but direct. Empowering without being saccharine. Uses tough love sparingly, encouragement often. Speaks AS IF sitting across from the reader at a café, not lecturing.

## Core voice dimensions

| Dimension | Value | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Formality | Low–medium | "Listen, you're not for everyone — and that's the best news ever!" |
| Address | Second person, nearly every sentence | "You've got big dreams, right?" |
| Authority | Earned, not claimed | "Here's the truth:" (introduces insight, not credentials) |
| Energy | High but not frantic | Mix of short punchy sentences + occasional longer ones for rhythm |
| Emotional register | Empathetic → challenging → uplifting | Problem → reframe → hope arc in every item |

## Structural patterns

### Item structure (applied to each of 12 numbered items)
```
{Heading: imperative, 3-7 words}
{Hook sentence: stark truth or myth-buster, 1 line}
{Paragraph 1: expose the problem/myth, 40-60 words}
{Paragraph 2: actionable reframe + where to focus, 40-60 words}
```

### Heading formulas (pick one per item, vary across the 12)
- `Stop {verb}ing {object}.` → "Stop chasing shiny objects."
- `Stop {verb} {object}.` → "Stop ignoring email."
- `Stop thinking {clause}.` → "Stop thinking you're not ready yet."
- `Stop being afraid to {verb}.` → "Stop being afraid to invest in yourself."
- `Stop saying "{phrase}."` → `Stop saying "I can't."`
- `Stop settling.` (pure imperative, no object — use once, for punch)

Rule: no two items share the same formula back-to-back. Vary rhythm.

### Opening hook page structure
```
{Title: rhetorical question or aspirational statement}
{Paragraph: empathetic problem diagnosis — "doesn't it feel like..."}
{Paragraph: name the hidden truth most people miss}
{Handwritten-accent line: transition into the list, e.g., "here's where it all starts:"}
{Summary of what follows}
```

## Sentence patterns (use liberally)

**Direct address openers:**
- "Listen, ..."
- "Look. ..."
- "Here's the truth: ..."
- "Here's something most {audience} never understand..."

**Myth-busters:**
- "The biggest lie you're telling yourself?"
- "{X} is a myth."
- "Not every {X} is {Y}."

**Reframes:**
- "The only way to get {X} is to {Y}."
- "It's not about {X} — it's about {Y}."
- "The key to {X} isn't {Y}; it's {Z}."

**Punchlines (short, standalone):**
- "Progress beats perfection every.damn.time."
- "Email is money, plain and simple."
- "A smaller, loyal audience is the goldmine."
- "Metrics are your map to growth."

**Aspirational closers (for intro + closing page):**
- "Everything you've been dreaming of is within reach..."
- "Let's make {year} your richest, most fulfilling year yet."

## Rhythm rules

1. **3-sentence rule**: no paragraph exceeds 3 sentences. If it does, break it.
2. **Short-long alternation**: punchy line → contextual line → punchy line. Don't stack 3 punchy in a row (feels robotic) or 3 long in a row (feels academic).
3. **One "em-dash moment" per paragraph max.** Em-dashes are drama — overuse dilutes the hit.
4. **Questions sparingly.** Max 1 rhetorical question per page. Reader fatigue sets in fast.

## Emoji conventions

Marie uses emojis as **structural markers**, not decoration.

| Emoji | Usage context |
|---|---|
| 👉 | Before a "hidden truth" statement ("👉 Success in business isn't just about what you DO...") |
| 💖 | Benefit/differentiator — "It's Proven:" |
| 💼 | Benefit/differentiator — "It's Action-Oriented:" |
| 🌍 | Benefit/differentiator — "It's Global and Inclusive:" |
| 💡 | Benefit/differentiator — "It's Risk-Free:" |
| 🌈 🦄 💸 🤸 ✨ | Benefit pills (Freedom / Prosperity / Happiness etc.) |

Rule: max **1 emoji per header/benefit**, zero emojis in item body paragraphs. Body text stays clean.

## Forbidden patterns (breaks the voice)

- ❌ Hedging: "maybe", "perhaps", "it might be" — too soft for this brand
- ❌ Academic transitions: "Furthermore", "Moreover", "In conclusion"
- ❌ AI-giveaway phrases: "In today's fast-paced world", "Let's dive in", "unpack", "game-changer" (overused), "unlock your potential" (cliché)
- ❌ Passive voice in headings
- ❌ More than 2 exclamation marks on a single page
- ❌ Code-switching Vietnamese ↔ English inside a single paragraph (unless brand voice explicitly uses it)
- ❌ Fake intimacy: "my friend", "beautiful soul", "rockstar" — feels pandering

## Vietnamese adaptation notes

When `language: vi`:

**Voice anchor pronouns**: Default to `em` (creator audience) addressed by `chị` author, OR `bạn` for neutral. Lock one choice and stick to it — **never mix**.

**Imperative formula mapping:**
- "Stop confusing X with Y" → "Đừng nhầm X với Y" or "Ngừng ngay việc nhầm X với Y"
- "Stop chasing shiny objects" → "Đừng chạy theo cái bóng hào nhoáng"
- "Stop thinking you're not ready" → "Đừng nghĩ là em chưa sẵn sàng"

**Power phrase Vietnamese equivalents:**
- "Progress beats perfection" → "Tiến bộ mỗi ngày đánh bại sự hoàn hảo ảo"
- "Email is money, plain and simple" → "Email là tiền, đơn giản vậy thôi"
- "Here's the truth" → "Sự thật là" / "Thực ra là"
- "Ready is a myth" → "Chuyện 'sẵn sàng' chỉ là ảo tưởng"

**Avoid**: English-Vietnamese mixing ("bạn cần focus vào...") unless the user's brand does it (check brand voice input). Default to clean Vietnamese.

**Avoid translation-ese**: Don't literal-translate English idioms. "Shiny objects" → "những thứ hào nhoáng/mới mẻ" (not "vật thể sáng bóng").

## Calibration check (run at Phase 5)

Before shipping, read 3 random items aloud. Check:
- [ ] Would a 30-year-old creator actually say this at a coffee shop?
- [ ] Is there ANY sentence that sounds like AI wrote it? (Flag: "in today's world", "unpack", "dive deep", "game-changer")
- [ ] Do items feel like they belong to the same author, not 12 different ones?
- [ ] Is there at least ONE line per item that would work as a tweet/quote card?

If any check fails, revise the offending item.
